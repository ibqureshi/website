PROJECTS

Orthopedic Surgery and Sports Medicine

I’ve had the privilege of working on projects that focus on sports injuries and orthopedic surgery across various institutions. My work has examined injury rates in professional sports leagues, with a particular interest in how specific variables like bye weeks, the use of turf fields, and unique events like the Winter Olympics affect injury outcomes.

  • Injury Rates in the National Hockey League (NHL): Analysis of injury patterns before and after the introduction of a midseason bye week.
  • Impact of the Winter Olympics on NHL Injuries: Investigating increased injury risks in years when NHL players participate in the Winter Olympics.
  • Turf vs. Grass in Major League Soccer (MLS): Assessing the injury rates on different playing surfaces in the MLS.

Unique Orthopedic Case Reports

Working under Dr. Mustafa Javed Bhalli, I have compiled case reports on unique orthopedic cases encountered in his international practice. These cases provide valuable insights into unusual presentations and treatment approaches.

  • Orthopedic Injuries During Ramadan: A project assessing injury risks among Muslim footballers during Ramadan, demonstrating that fasting does not necessarily increase injury risk.

Female Authorship and Trends in Orthopedic Journals

As part of an ongoing initiative, I’ve been involved in evaluating female authorship trends in leading orthopedic journals. This project sheds light on the representation of female researchers in the field and its evolution over time.

  • Authorship Trends in Orthopedic Journals (2018-2023): Analyzing female first and senior author rates in top orthopedic publications across different regions.

Geriatric Orthopedics and Multidisciplinary Care

I’ve contributed to reviews focusing on geriatric patients, particularly on how multidisciplinary care can improve outcomes in older adults with rib fractures.

  • Rib Fracture Management in Older Adults: A scoping review that explores best practices for managing rib fractures in the geriatric population.
